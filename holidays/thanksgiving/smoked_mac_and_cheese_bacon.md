# Smoked Mac & Cheese with Bacon

## Context

- Holiday: Thanksgiving
- Smoker: Pit Boss Austin XL
- Cook Style: Smoke
- Wood: Apple or mild blend

## Ingredients

- 1 lb elbow macaroni
- 4 tbsp butter
- 4 tbsp flour
- 2½ cups whole milk
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 2 cups sharp cheddar (shredded)
- 2 cups gouda blend (shredded)
- 1 lb bacon, cooked and chopped
- Salt & black pepper to taste
- Optional: light paprika or cayenne (very small amount)

## Prep

1. Cook pasta to **just under al dente**. Drain.
2. Cook bacon until crisp. Chop.
3. Make roux: melt butter, whisk in flour, cook 1–2 minutes.
4. Slowly whisk in milk and cream until smooth.
5. Add cheeses gradually until melted.
6. Fold in pasta and bacon.

## Cook

- Temp: **250°F**
- Time: **60–90 minutes**
- Pan: Aluminum or cast iron
- Stir once around the 45-minute mark

## Notes

- This dish absorbs smoke quickly — keep smoke light.
- If top begins to dry, cover loosely with foil.
- Uncover for final 10–15 minutes to set the top.

## Feedback & Ratings

- ⭐ Rating (1–5): 5
- Would make again? Yes
- What worked well: Gouda blend + bacon balance; creamy texture held up in smoker
- Improvements: None noted
- Variations / recommendations: Keep smoke mild; don’t overcook pasta
